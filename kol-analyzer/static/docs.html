01

Overview

KOL Terminal analyzes crypto Twitter accounts to help you figure out who's actually worth listening to and who's full of it. We crawl tweets, run them through multiple analysis modules, and spit out a credibility score.

The system uses a multi-layered approach: 13 different analyzers examine different aspects of an account's behavior, from engagement patterns to consistency to linguistic manipulation tactics. Each module produces a score from 0-100, which are then weighted and combined into an overall credibility score.

How it works: We fetch recent tweets (up to 1000), run them through our analysis pipeline, and aggregate the results. The more tweets we can analyze, the higher our confidence in the results.

02

The 13 Analysis Modules

Each module examines a specific aspect of the account's behavior. They run in parallel and contribute to the final score based on their assigned weights.

Reputation
weight: 11%
What others say about this person. Highest weighted because community consensus matters.
Consistency
weight: 9%
Do they flip positions on tokens? Tracks sentiment history per ticker and detects reversals.
Baiting
weight: 9%
FOMO tactics, engagement farming, reward gaming, and manipulation patterns.
Prediction
weight: 9%
Track record on past calls. Did their predictions actually pan out?
Privilege
weight: 8%
"High horse" moralism and empathy levels. Are they punching down on others?
Engagement
weight: 7%
Bot detection via engagement variance, like-reply ratios, and posting patterns.
Dissonance
weight: 7%
Hypocrisy and tone inconsistency. Do they mock others for things they've done themselves?
Transparency
weight: 7%
Sponsored content disclosure rates. Do they mark their paid shills?
Follower Quality
weight: 7%
Estimates bot followers from engagement patterns and statistical signals.
Temporal
weight: 7%
Timing vs price action. Are they front-running their audience?
Linguistic
weight: 7%
Language authenticity and manipulation tactics detection.
Accountability
weight: 7%
Do they own their Ls or cherry-pick wins? Responsibility and honesty.
Network
weight: 5%
Reply guy behavior and interaction patterns with other accounts.
03

Contradiction Detection (BS Meter)

The BS Meter specifically tracks flip-flops and contradictions. It looks for cases where someone said one thing, then did or said the opposite. This is separate from the main credibility score but gives you a quick read on consistency.

We detect four types of contradictions:

1
Holding Contradictionshigh impact
Said "diamond hands" or "never selling" on a token, then sold within days. Detected by matching holding language ("hodl", "holding long term", "never sell") with selling language ("sold", "dumped", "exited", "took profits") on the same ticker.
2
Sentiment Contradictionshigh impact
Flipped from bullish to bearish (or vice versa) on the same token. Uses keyword lists for bullish ("moon", "100x", "accumulating") and bearish ("dump", "crash", "avoid", "dead") sentiment.
3
Promotion Contradictionsmedium impact
Claimed content was "organic" or "not paid" but also posted clearly sponsored content (#ad, #sponsored). Flags within a 90-day window.
4
Advice Contradictionsmedium impact
Said "NFA" (not financial advice) but then gave direct trading orders like "buy now", "sell immediately", or made "guaranteed" claims.

Severity is based on timing:

High severity: Contradiction within 7 days (holding) or 3 days (sentiment)
Medium severity: Contradiction within 30 days (holding) or 14 days (sentiment)
Low severity: Longer timeframes or less impactful contradiction types

BS Score Formula: weighted_score = (high_count x 10) + (medium_count x 5) + (low_count x 2) bs_score = min(100, (weighted_score / max(tweets/100, 1)) x 5) Example: 2 high + 3 medium contradictions on 200 tweets = ~35 BS score
04

Scoring and Grades

The final credibility score is a weighted sum of all 13 module scores. Each module contributes based on its assigned weight, and the result is a score from 0-100.

Overall Score = sum of (module_score x module_weight) for all 13 modules Weights sum to 100%: Reputation (11%) + Consistency (9%) + Baiting (9%) + Prediction (9%) + Privilege (8%) + Engagement (7%) + Dissonance (7%) + Transparency (7%) + Follower Quality (7%) + Temporal (7%) + Linguistic (7%) + Accountability (7%) + Network (5%) = 100%

Grades are assigned based on score thresholds:

grade score range what it means
A 85-100 Actually decent. Rare to find.
B 70-84 Not completely mid. Above average.
C 55-69 Mid. As expected for most accounts.
D 40-54 Kinda sus. Proceed with caution.
F 0-39 Certified clown. Probably avoid.

Confidence score: We also provide a confidence percentage based on how much data we have. More tweets = higher confidence. The confidence is calculated from tweet count (50%), follower count (30%), and a base value (20%).

Archetypes and Trust Levels

Based on the analysis results, each account is classified into an archetype that summarizes their typical behavior pattern. This gives you a quick read on what kind of account you're dealing with.

Trust levels provide an actionable recommendation:

+
Higher Trust
Consistently good track record, transparent about sponsorships, owns their mistakes. Worth following for actual insight.
~
Consider / Neutral
Mixed signals or not enough data. Do your own research on specific claims they make.
!

Some red flags or primarily entertainment value. Don't base financial decisions on their content.
x
Avoid
Multiple serious red flags. History of manipulation, scams, or consistent misleading content.
06

Limitations

This tool is meant to be a starting point, not the final word. Here's what you should know:

Not financial advice. This is an analysis tool, not a recommendation engine. Always do your own research before making any financial decisions.

Data limitations: We can only analyze public tweets. Private accounts, deleted tweets, and off-platform behavior aren't captured. The analysis is only as good as the data we can access.

Context matters: Automated analysis can miss nuance, sarcasm, or legitimate changes in opinion based on new information. A "contradiction" might actually be someone updating their view based on new data.

Gaming potential: Like any metric, this can potentially be gamed by sophisticated actors who understand the signals we look for. Use it as one input among many.

Point in time: Results reflect behavior up to the analysis date. People can change. A bad score from a year ago might not reflect current behavior.
